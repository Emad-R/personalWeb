Early Life

I was born and raised in Sylhet, Bangladesh - a third-world country. Needless to say, poverty and famine reigned through the cities and villages. Infrastructure as a whole was either dilapidated or flat-out neglected. The country not only suffered economical and political quandaries, but also, unnecessary belligerence: murder, robbery, assaults, and corruption. All too common. However, despite these predicaments, it didn't exactly eliminate the thrill and fun, especially in the countryside. It is because of a lack of technology that we bonded with nature and amongst ourselves.

Culture

Growing up in Bangladesh, the majority of the people devoted their religious faith to Islam. My family and I are in that category, and like other religions, we have certain rules and beliefs that must be followed. The principles of Islam can be broken down into five pillars: Shahada (confession of faith), Salat (prayer), Zakat (charity), sawm (fasting), and Hajj (pilgrimage to Mecca, if financially possible). These are the major criteria of a Muslim person. It should be noted that Islam is a religion that strictly supports peace and harmony amongst mankind.
